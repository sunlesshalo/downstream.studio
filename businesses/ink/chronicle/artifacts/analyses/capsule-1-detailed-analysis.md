**Scroll range:** 3800-5700px
**Duration:** 1900px
**Color:** COLORED
**Intensity:** HIGH

**Visual Composition:**
- Primary subject: Canary standing ON the cage (not inside it — surreal placement)
- Secondary elements: The cage itself, a lantern
- Background: Mine wall — grounded, specific place (canary in the mine reference)
- Multiple elements working together, not single subject

**Color:**
- Subject: Golden amber/yellow canary
- Background: Dark brown/black mine wall
- Temperature: WARM accent in dark void
- **COLOR SHIFT:** Yellow/gold → Amber/Orange → Red during scene

**Motion:**
- Camera: FIXED (no movement)
- Subject: Bird MOVES HEAD realistically, BREATHES
- The movement is subtle but continuous — draws the gaze
- Very realistic movement on a painted/drawn subject

**The Magic:**
- Painted/drawn style (not photorealistic)
- But movement is VERY REALISTIC (head turn, breathing)
- Plus SURREAL color shift (yellow → red)
- The contrast between these three creates tension and wonder

**Color shift detail:** The bird keeps moving during the whole scene. The color change makes the breathing even more explicit. The transformation is stunning — gradual shift through the scroll.

## PRODUCTION INSIGHTS

### 1. Front-Load the Effort

| Phase | Scenes | Effort Level |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Hook + Establish | 1-3 | HIGH (color, complex motion) |
| Develop | 4-5 | MEDIUM-HIGH (multi-directional, state change) |
| Coast | 6-8 | LOW-MEDIUM (simple motion, muted colors) |
| Close | 9 | MINIMAL (CTA) |

**Why:** Reader is hooked by scene 3. Don't need to maintain max effort throughout. Save tokens/cost on later scenes.

### 2. Color Rhythm

| Pattern | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Colored → B&W → Colored → B&W → Colored | First 5 scenes alternate |
| Then all muted/grey | Scenes 6-9 |

Creates visual rhythm, prevents monotony in first half. Second half can be simpler.

### 3. Motion Variety

Never repeat the same motion strategy in consecutive scenes:
1. Camera + Subject
2. Camera only
3. Subject only
4. Multi-directional
5. State change
6. Camera only
7. Interactive
8. Environment only
9. Static

### 4. Multiple Elements Per Scene

Every scene has at least 2-3 elements, not single subjects:
- Scene 1: Bird + clouds
- Scene 2: Cityscape + texture overlay
- Scene 3: Canary + cage + lantern + mine wall
- Scene 4: Two figures + hill + debris + clouds
- Scene 5: Multiple cages + lights + birds/feathers
- Scene 6: Two people + desks + photos
- Scene 7: Door + human + light
- Scene 8: Two silhouettes + cityscape + debris + hearts

### 5. The Interactive Trick

Scene 7 uses the door as subject. Door responds to scroll. This prompts viewer to scroll up/down to play. Creates engagement.

**Applicable to:** Doors, windows, eyes, curtains, books — anything that can open/close.

### 6. State Changes

Scenes 5 and 8 have elements that APPEAR during the scene:
- Scene 5: Lights turn on, birds emerge, elements rise
- Scene 8: Hearts appear on silhouettes

These are not in the initial keyframe — they emerge through the scroll.

### 7. Breathing as Life

Multiple scenes have "breathing" elements:
- Scene 3: Canary breathes
- Scene 7: Human silhouette breathes

Subtle, continuous motion that adds life to otherwise static elements.
